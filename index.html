The Lord's Prayer

Today we will be praying with the Our Father. In Matthew's gospel, Jesus introduces with the instruction: "This is how you are to pray." Think about how many times you have said this prayer from memory without thinking much about what you are saying. Savor this opportunity to go deep on the words, meaning, and source of the Lord's Prayer. If this is your first time seeing the Our Father, what a place to journey towards a deeper relationship with God.

Imagine yourself in a scene like those below. Then read passage.

"This is how you are to pray: Our Father in heaven, hallowed be your name, your kingdom come, your will be done, on earth as in heaven. Give us today our daily bread; and forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors; and do not subject us to the final test, but deliver us from the evil one."

-Jesus (Matthew 6: 9-13)

Meditate and contemplate

Re-read the passage and reflect on what stood out to you. what might the Lord be trying to tell you?
